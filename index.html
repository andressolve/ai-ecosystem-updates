geopolitical US-China Export Controls Tightening

(a) US reportedly drafting rules requiring government approval to ship AI chips anywhere outside the US. (TechCrunch, Mar 5)

(b) H200 exports to China capped at 75,000 units per company — well below ~2M chip orders (~$54B gross) from Chinese firms.

(c) China announces massive AI/semiconductor subsidy program (Mar 6) — doubling down on self-sufficiency.

(d) Trump-Xi summit scheduled March 31–April 2 — export policy under review ahead of visit.

Bull
Domestic self-sufficiency programs historically fail on timeline. Huawei Ascend still generation(s) behind on perf/watt. Universal export control proposal is politically extreme.
Bear
H200 cap is directly bearish for NVIDIA near-term China revenue. The ~$40B net China estimate may need significant downward revision. Regulatory uncertainty overhang persists.

policy White House Ratepayer Protection Pledge

Amazon, Google, Meta, Microsoft, OpenAI, Oracle, and xAI signed the pledge on March 4–5. Commitments:

  • Self-fund all new power generation for data centers
  • Pay for all power delivery infrastructure upgrades — whether they use the electricity or not
  • Negotiate separate rate structures with utilities and state governments
  • Invest in local communities: hiring, skills programs, grid reliability

Thesis impact: This is the political green light for the AI infrastructure buildout. Revealed preference — 7 companies wouldn’t commit to self-funding power if they expected demand to soften. Added to computational abundance supporting evidence.

Bull
Removes political friction. Accelerates permitting. Buildout proceeds at planned pace.
Bear
“Bring your own power” adds material cost ($10B+ per company). 49 GW projected shortfall by 2028 remains the physical constraint regardless of who pays.
