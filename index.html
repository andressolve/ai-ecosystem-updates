EarningsP022 RESOLVED YES 1. NVDA Q1 FY27 Print — Beats Across the Board

Headline: Revenue $81.62B (+5.6% QoQ, vs UBS $80.6B / Street $78.8B / guide $78B ±2%). DC $76.4B; DC Compute $61.6B; Networking $14.8B (+199% YoY); GM 75.0% (held); EPS $1.84 (vs Street $1.75).

Q2 FY27 guide: Revenue $91.0B ±2% — TOP of pre-print bogey ($90-91B), Street was $86.9B. GM 75.0% ±50bps. Opex 38-40% of GM. Capital return: +$80B buyback authorization, dividend hiked 25x.

Five analytical reads:

  1. Networking +199% YoY ($14.8B) is the answer to the SemiA pricing-power critique. NVDA captures spread via platform (NVLink, Spectrum-X, BlueField) not chip pricing. Networking is now ~18% of DC and growing faster than Compute — exactly the "platform extension" answer to the 5/1 SemiAnalysis frame.
  2. GM held at 75% during Rubin mix-in directly resolves UBS pre-call margin compression concern. Rubin ramp is NOT margin-dilutive at the gross-margin line in Q1; UBS modeled compression that did not materialize.
  3. $80B buyback addition = MSFT-style capital-return regime. Implies ~$50-60B/yr repo cadence on top of organic ROIC. Multiple-expanding lever previously absent from sell-side models.
  4. Vera CPU $200B TAM disclosed — biggest forward-narrative shift. CPU is no longer a free-throw-in to GPU systems; Vera is being sold as standalone DC compute, attacking Intel/AMD x86 directly. Confirms 5/19 CPU-demand special report analysis that mix-shift x86→ARM/Vera is real.
  5. Computational abundance hypothesis — triple-axis confirmation in single print: (a) aggregate demand: $81.6B exceeds the most aggressive sell-side (UBS); (b) TAM widening: Vera $200B; (c) pricing structure intact at 75% GM during multi-generation transition. All three vectors fire simultaneously.

Source: NVIDIA Q1 FY27 release, 5/20 AMC.
