What Happened

bear Disconfirming (watch): DRAM price-fixing class action filed vs Samsung + SK Hynix + Micron

A federal class action was filed June 25 in the Northern District of California naming all three DRAM makers. 17 plaintiffs (individuals + small businesses) allege the trio colluded to keep commodity DRAM (DDR3/DDR4) artificially scarce — framing the marketed "HBM pivot" as cover to curtail commodity supply — driving ~700% price inflation over a four-year span. The complaint leans on the 2000s DOJ price-fixing matter (Samsung and SK Hynix guilty pleas, ~$731M combined fines, executives jailed) as a recidivism pattern. As of 6/30: no admissions, no settlements; earliest stage.

Why it matters — and why it's bounded. This does not disconfirm AI compute demand or the demand-driven memory super-cycle. The memory predictions all hold (P015 95%, P028 55%, P029 60%, P030 90%) — civil class actions run in years, there's no DOJ involvement, and no maker has disclosed a reserve. What it is: the political-economy / value-capture bear case (sub-thesis #5 affordability) made concrete. The allegation cuts both ways for the framework:

  • Against the makers (the "market"): if it ever drew DOJ/FTC attention, it's a margin/overhang risk to the memory suppliers — distinct from whether the underlying AI demand is real.
  • For the thesis (the "world"): the makers' defense is precisely that the shortage is structural — fab space + EUV lead times, with the whole complex (including non-defendant capacity) tight. This update's own SK Hynix/Micron data corroborate genuine AI-led undersupply, not manufactured scarcity.
