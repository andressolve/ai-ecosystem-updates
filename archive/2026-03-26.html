What Happened

1. DISCONFIRMING (Weak-Moderate) — Google TurboQuant: 6x KV Cache Compression Rattles Memory Stocks disconfirming memory

Google Research released TurboQuant (ICLR 2026) — compresses LLM KV cache from 16 bits to 3 bits. 6x memory reduction, 8x inference speedup on H100. Zero accuracy loss.

MetricImpactScope
KV cache compression16-bit → 3-bit (6x reduction)Inference only. ~15-25% of total inference GPU memory.
Inference speedup8x on H100Throughput per GPU increases significantly.
Training memoryUnaffectedMain HBM demand driver is untouched.
Model weightsUnaffectedThe bulk of GPU memory usage remains unchanged.

Market reaction: MU -5.2%, WDC -4.7%, SNDK -5.7%, SK Hynix -6.3% post-market.

Our assessment: Market overreaction. The sell-off is a sentiment event, not a fundamental threat.

  • KV cache is ~15-25% of total inference GPU memory. Model weights (the bulk) are unaffected.
  • Training memory (the main HBM demand driver) is completely unaffected.
  • NVIDIA's own response: freed memory → longer context windows + higher batch sizes = more inference throughput per GPU. This is textbook Jevons paradox.
  • Community ported to MLX and llama.cpp within 24 hours — adoption propagates efficiency into more use cases.
  • DRAM undersupply (P015) driven by fab space and equipment lead times, not inference efficiency.
Jevons paradox (likely) Freed inference memory enables longer contexts + higher batch sizes = more total inference compute consumed. Every past efficiency gain in AI has increased total demand, not reduced it. Google itself benefits — lower serving costs per query = more queries served.
Efficiency outpaces demand (tail risk) If compression techniques compound (KV cache + weight quantization + architecture improvements), the total GPU fleet needed for a given workload shrinks. If demand doesn't grow fast enough to absorb, memory stocks face multiple compression.

Filed as disconfirming (Weak-Moderate) for computational abundance thesis. Mechanism favors Jevons paradox holding. Source: Google Research / ICLR 2026, March 25-26, 2026

  • Compute scarcity signal: Even the world's largest AI lab can't serve all demand vectors simultaneously. Strategic triage reveals demand exceeds supply within a single organization.
  • Value hierarchy: "Useful" AI (coding, reasoning) winning over "impressive" AI (video generation). Revenue per FLOP drives allocation.
  • Anthropic confirmed at $19B ARR (from $9B at YE2025). Claude Code alone at $2.5B ARR. Enterprise AI revenue doubling in months.
Demand > supply at company level If the best-resourced AI lab in the world has to shut down a product line to serve higher-value demand, compute is genuinely scarce. This is the strongest single-company evidence for the abundance thesis — we're nowhere near abundance yet.
