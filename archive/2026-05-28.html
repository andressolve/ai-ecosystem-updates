Agentic CPU Snowflake commits $6B / 5-yr to AWS Graviton — first production-scale agentic-CPU validation

Announced 5/27 PM alongside Snowflake FQ1 earnings (product revenue $1.39B, +33% YoY; FY guide raised to $5.84B). Stock +38% AH to $241.65. WSJ: "Cloud-storage company joins Apple, Meta as one of AWS's largest customers for CPU-based computing."

Why it matters: Direct empirical confirmation of the agentic-CPU thesis logged in the 5/19 CPU demand special report. Snowflake explicitly framed Graviton as the agentic-workload compute (data shuttling, retrieval, orchestration, agent runtime) — separate from inference acceleration (GPU/ASIC). Three of AWS's largest CPU customers now public: Apple, Meta, Snowflake. The "AI demand expands to CPU layer" mechanism is visible at production scale, not just NVDA Grace/Vera framing.

Cross-stack reads: (a) Reinforces 4/9 Jassy disclosure (>$20B chip run-rate, "two large customers want our entire Graviton capacity"). (b) Negative read-through to INTC (Snowflake was Xeon-heavy) and modestly to AMD (EPYC partial migration path). (c) Validates "software-not-disrupted-by-AI" view — Snowflake is the cleanest counterexample to PLTR-style "AI eats SaaS."

Sources: AWS PR via IBKR DJ-RT 5/28; CNBC, WSJ, Barron's coverage.
