What Happened

1. NVIDIA Halts H200 Production — China Pipeline Dead critical

NVIDIA has ended H200 production entirely, redirecting all TSMC wafer capacity to the Vera Rubin platform. The ~$54B gross / ~$40B net China pipeline is effectively dead.

  • 250,000 H200 chips already produced sitting in storage — no buyer, no shipments
  • Zero chips shipped to China despite January license approval. Commerce Dept confirmed none sold.
  • China blocking at customs + building domestic stack (Huawei Ascend + DeepSeek V4 optimized for Ascend)
  • Western Vera Rubin demand is the pull factor: MSFT deploying NVL72, CoreWeave/AWS/GCP all scheduled H2 2026
Bull: Vera Rubin capacity accelerated. Higher ASPs, better margins, committed demand. $78B Q1 guide already excluded China — near-term unaffected. Frees Jensen to pivot GTC messaging entirely to next-gen.
Bear: Removes $8-12B FY27 China optionality permanently. 250K chips in storage = dead inventory. Global compute ecosystem bifurcation shrinks addressable market for US chipmakers. China's parallel AI stack becomes self-sustaining.

Sources: DigiTimes, Yahoo Finance, Proactive Investors

2. Amazon Record ~$50B Bond Sale — Financial System Underwrites AI Buildout major

Amazon launched the largest non-M&A corporate bond sale in history to fund its ~$200B 2026 capex guide for AI infrastructure.

  • $37-42B in 11-part US bonds + ~€10B (~$11.6B) record euro bonds = ~$50B total
  • $126B in investor orders — 3.4x oversubscribed
  • Fourth-largest US corporate bond ever; largest not tied to an acquisition
  • Amazon's $84B cash reserves insufficient for capex plan — debt now structural
Bull: Bond market — most conservative capital allocator — is aggressively underwriting AI infrastructure. 3.4x oversubscription = institutional credit market wants MORE AI exposure. Strongest financial commitment signal for compute abundance thesis. "Utility-scale compute financing" thesis validated.
Bear: Leverage increases balance sheet vulnerability. If 4:1 capex-to-revenue ratio doesn't improve by 2027-2028, debt service becomes a constraint. Structural shift from internally-funded to levered = higher risk profile.

Sources: Bloomberg, Benzinga, Yahoo Finance

3. Meta Scraps MTIA Training Chip — NVIDIA Training Moat Validated significant

Meta cancelled its advanced MTIA training chip (codename "Olympus"). Design complexity and software instability were the primary reasons. Inference-only MTIA-3 continues on track.

  • v5 "Olympus" (training + inference) and v6 "Universal Core" (full-stack) programs cancelled
  • Executives judged proceeding risked the AI model race against OpenAI and Google
  • Meta doubling down on external: AMD MI450 ($100B+, 6 GW) + NVIDIA Rubin mega-deal + Google TPU rental
  • MTIA-3 for inference on track (TSMC 3nm, H2 2026, 35% fleet target unchanged)
Bull for NVIDIA: Meta — with $135B capex and maximum incentive — could not build a training chip. NVIDIA training moat is wider than assumed. Training silicon difficulty bar (CUDA software stack, NVLink interconnect, compiler investment) higher than market expected. NVIDIA dependency at Meta is now permanent for training.
Nuance: Google's TPU success shows custom training silicon IS possible — it just requires a multi-decade, Google-scale investment. Anthropic on TPU+Trainium remains the primary custom silicon training risk. Custom silicon is bifurcating: training = NVIDIA/Google; inference = fragmenting.

Sources: Dataconomy, The Information, GuruFocus

4. Oil Price Extreme Volatility — Brent Swings Between $80-$91 macro

Oil markets in extreme volatility tied to the Iran/Strait of Hormuz crisis, with conflicting signals creating wild swings.

  • Brent plunged 17% below $80/bbl on Monday — steepest single-day drop in years
  • Rebounded to ~$91 after (false) Energy Secretary claim re: Navy escort through Strait
  • IEA considering largest oil reserve release in history (WSJ)
  • Currently ~$91/bbl — down from ~$98-99 pre-crash but highly unstable
Bull for buildout: Net directional move (down from ~$99 to $91) reduces energy cost headwind for data center deployment. If IEA reserves release + Strait de-escalation sustain lower prices, DC economics improve meaningfully.
Bear: Underlying physical constraint is power availability, not power cost. Extreme volatility makes long-term power cost planning unreliable. Strait disruption ongoing — could reverse.

Sources: Fortune, CNBC, Al Jazeera
