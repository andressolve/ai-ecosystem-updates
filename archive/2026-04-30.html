What Happened

1. Sell-side response is the dominant story — splits cleanly along three axes Big signal

The PT actions across Wall Street post-AMC are, taken together, the most informative single data point in the cycle. Three patterns:

  • Axis A — GOOG / AMZN: universal raises, often by 15–35%. TD Cowen lifted GOOGL by $75 in a single move (+20%); Benchmark raised AMZN by $95 (+35%). Synchronized model rebuild — the Street is re-rating the cloud businesses. GOOG closed at ATH.
  • Axis B — META: bifurcated. Bulls raised (Evercore $900→$930), bears cut hard (Cantor $850→$750, Truist $900→$840). JPM downgraded to Neutral. Real analytical disagreement, not consensus — capex intensity racing ahead of monetization is contested.
  • Axis C — MSFT: bifurcated DIFFERENTLY. Multiple PT cuts of $50–100 co-existed with raises. Mean drift roughly flat to slightly negative — but dispersion widened. The "AI winner → AI loser" Heard-on-the-Street narrative is gaining traction even after the print: $190B capex was a shock, OpenAI revenue-share end leaves a modeling void, and Azure 40% was the slowest of the three big clouds (vs. GOOG Cloud 63%, AWS 28% on a much larger base).

Implication: the market is moving from "all hyperscalers benefit equally" to differentiated re-rating. GOOG and AMZN are getting credit for AI-cloud monetization that's now visible (GOOG Cloud OM 32.9%, AWS re-acceleration to 15-quarter high). MSFT's monetization story is murkier post-OpenAI restructuring. META's question is "will Reality Labs ever pay back?"

Thesis composition: comp-abundance intact and strengthening, but the value-accrual sub-question is bifurcating in real time. Per CLAUDE.md risk #5 ("value accrues elsewhere"), this is exactly the disaggregation we should track.

2. Samsung Q1 — the memory super-boom is now categorical Strong supporting

Samsung Q1 2026: revenue 133.9T won (~$90B), operating profit 57.2T won (~$40B, +750% YoY). Memory operating profit ~$33.6B. Chip profit up 48× YoY.

  • Demand fulfillment rate at record low — Samsung is supply-constrained on memory.
  • Customers pulling forward 2027 demand.
  • Company "warns of 2027 shortage" widening, not narrowing.
  • HBM4 mass production for Vera Rubin began Feb 2026; HBM revenue tracking >3× for 2026.
  • Conventional DRAM more profitable than HBM right now — quarterly negotiation cycle vs annual HBM contracts. Spot leading; HBM repriced in 2027.

Cleanest single data point that the memory layer is structurally undersupplied for two more years.

  • P015 ("DRAM undersupply through C3Q26") — needs extension. Samsung's own guidance suggests undersupply through 2027 minimum.
  • WSJ: "AI Has Made Memory Chips One of the World's Most Profitable Products" — Q1 prices were expected to rise 50% QoQ; "got better — a lot better."
  • MSFT's $25B component-pricing uplift (~13% of $190B 2026 capex) is the demand-side echo.
  • Micron read-through: FQ3 guide ($33.5B at 81% GM) looks more credible, possibly conservative. P021 (MU FQ3 ≥$33B at 88%) bias upward.

3. Brent at 4-year high on US-Iran kinetic risk — P008 oil-driven trim back on the table Macro

Brent crossed $126/bbl intraday before pulling back to ~$114 — highest since 2022. Drivers:

  • US and Iran reportedly blockading Strait of Hormuz (~30% of seaborne oil)
  • Trump publicly considering military action; rejected Iran proposal Sunday
  • UAE leaves OPEC effective tomorrow 5/1 (3rd-largest producer)
  • IEA: "largest supply shock on record"

Critical timing: the 4 hyperscalers reaffirmed/raised capex before this further oil leg up. The 0/4 energy-cite tells us about willingness to commit at $100–110, not at $125. If Brent holds $110+ for 30+ days OR a kinetic event materializes, hyperscalers in Q2 prints would face a different question.

P008 sub-tiers:

  • Hyperscaler reaffirms w/o energy-cite at <$110 oil → 70% (current)
  • Sustained $110–120 oil into Q2 prints with no capex revision → maintain 70%
  • Kinetic event (US strike, Hormuz fully shut > 60d) → 55–58%
  • Iran deal in 30 days → 75%

Bull case for the thesis: Samsung is willing to call out a 2027 shortage while oil is at $125. That's revealed preference — the data centers are getting built regardless.

7. Helium shortage from Middle East fighting — chip mfg input risk New / track

Barron's 4/30: "fighting in the Middle East has knocked roughly a third of the world's supply [of helium] offline." Helium normally trades at ~$500/Mcf, critical to semi mfg (cryogenic cooling for ion implantation, etching, lithography). Significant price spike expected.

Stack-layer implication: A potential physical-layer constraint on chip output that is not CoWoS, not HBM, not power. Adds to the supply ceiling concept under comp-abundance. Not yet a thesis-changing item.
