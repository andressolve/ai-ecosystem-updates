What Happened

bull Alphabet raising $84.75B in equity for AI infrastructure — Berkshire takes $10B

Alphabet announced (6/1, upsized 6/2) a set of equity offerings to fund "world-class AI compute infrastructure to meet unprecedented customer demand." Structure (CNBC, TechCrunch, SEC 424B5):

  • $30B underwritten public offering — $15B mandatory convertible preferred + $15B Class A/C common.
  • $40B at-the-market (ATM) program — expected to begin Q3 2026.
  • $10B private placement to Berkshire Hathaway — $5B Class A @ $351.81, $5B Class C @ $348.20.
  • Upsized 6/2 to a total ~$84.75B equity raise.

Why it matters (computational-abundance read — Strong supporting): This is a revealed-preference signal about the magnitude of compute demand. Alphabet generates ~$75B FCF/yr, holds $95B+ in cash, and already raised €9B + C$9.5B in debt last month (5/11). For it to now raise $85B in equity — the most dilutive, most expensive funding channel — to fund the buildout is a statement that capital intensity has outrun even the strongest hyperscaler balance sheet. It is the financing layer confirming the demand layer: the buildout is accelerating beyond what 2025 capex models priced, exactly the structural-underestimate the thesis predicts. Berkshire's $10B is the notable validation — the most famous value/AI-skeptical institution putting capital directly into AI-infrastructure equity at a ~6% discount. This extends the financing-layer pattern (AVGO/Apollo-Blackstone $35B private credit, Anthropic ~$65B equity, OpenAI IPO, Meta/Oracle/CoreWeave debt): every capital channel is now being tapped to fund AI infra simultaneously.

The counterweight (don't bury it — sub-thesis #5, value accrual / capital intensity): The market read it as a negative (GOOGL −3.86% on 6/2). Three real bear points: (1) Dilution + signal that internal FCF can't cover it — you issue $85B of equity when the buildout exceeds cash generation, which compresses per-share economics. (2) ROIC compression — our 2/17 capex-sustainability analysis already tracked Google ROIC falling 63% → 34% ↓; layering $85B fresh equity on top of $185–190B capex makes the return math harder, not easier. (3) Equity-issuance-near-peak risk — large equity raises tend to cluster near cycle tops, when management views the stock as a good currency. So the datapoint cuts both ways: it validates demand magnitude (bull) while raising the who-earns-an-adequate-return question (bear). Demand confirmed; value-accrual stays live.
