What Happened

Disconfirming Power/grid risk is now specific

Wood Mackenzie, summarized by ITPro, warned that AI data-center load is outrunning grid buildout. Transmission build-outs are 5-10 years away, PJM has 78 GW of committed data-center load versus 36 GW of accredited generation pipeline, and more than 90 GW of colocated generation in US interconnection queues carries meaningful technical/regulatory risk.

Read: not a demand problem; a deployment-pace problem. The clean bull assumption that hyperscalers can simply self-fund around grid limits is too easy.

Source: ITPro / Wood Mackenzie summary

App Layer Google I/O confirms distribution advantage

Google says AI Mode has surpassed 1B monthly users, AI Mode queries are more than doubling quarterly, and Search agents / Gemini Spark / Daily Brief are rolling into Search, Gemini, Gmail, Calendar, and Workspace surfaces.

Read: GOOG has the best agent-distribution surface in consumer internet. But Spark is still controlled beta/trusted tester territory, so this is not proof of production-scale autonomous-agent economics yet.

Source: Google I/O 2026 official recap

Networking Arista Q1 validates Ethernet AI fabric

Q1 revenue was $2.709B, +35.1% YoY. Non-GAAP operating margin held at 47.8%, operating cash flow was $1.69B, and Q2 revenue guide is about $2.8B.

Caveat: non-GAAP gross margin was 62.4% versus 64.1% in Q1 2025. Arista is converting demand, but hyperscaler/cloud mix pressures margins.

Source: Arista Q1 results

Second Source AMD invests like MI450/Helios is real

AMD announced more than $10B of Taiwan ecosystem investment and a Venice EPYC production ramp on TSMC 2nm.

Read: not an immediate NVIDIA killer, but it raises confidence that AMD will be a credible 2H26+ second-source AI platform if ROCm/software execution follows.

Sources: AMD Taiwan investment, Venice N2 ramp

Geopolitics NVIDIA China is two-sided

AP reported Taiwan prosecutors investigating alleged smuggling of NVIDIA-containing servers to China. Reuters Connect video showed Jensen Huang saying the $200B Vera CPU market framing includes China.

Read: China demand persists, but compliance risk is rising. FY27 estimates unchanged because China data-center compute was already modeled near zero.

Sources: AP Taiwan investigation, Reuters Connect
