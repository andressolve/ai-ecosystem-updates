Why the LT ROE step is the most important number

UBS no longer models Hynix as a deep-cyclical memory commodity name with peak ROE at one point in the cycle — they are now modeling a structurally higher through-cycle ROE, justified by HBM consuming 25-31% of industry DRAM wafer capacity through 2027. 5. The Three Pillars of the Bull Case

Pillar 1 — Memory undersupply continues through 2028
"Agentic AI is boosting further memory demand on multiple fronts beyond HBM, including DDR5/LPDDR5 for conventional servers and CPU head nodes in AI servers, as well as NAND flash for KV cache and storage. We now forecast an acceleration of memory demand growth into 2027 with DRAM bit end-consumption growing 35% YoY vs 21% in 2026, and NAND flash 23% from 20%. As almost all of the incremental wafer capacity addition for DRAM is going to HBM, and no new capacity ex China is being added to NAND, supply is not catching up with demand. The main risk is affordability as memory industry revenues are approaching US$1.4tn in 2027."

The strongest binding-constraint framing in any sell-side note to date. DRAM bit-demand growth accelerates rather than normalizing.

Pillar 2 — First-revised LTAs finalized (structural pricing visibility)

~25% of DRAM bits and ~20% of NAND bits fixed via 3-year LTAs. DDR5 AND NAND both in scope. Hynix is capacity-constrained vs Samsung until Yongin Y1 starts equipment Feb 2027.

LTAs reduce the cyclical character of the business by locking pricing floor for 25% of volume over 3 years. Combined with the 5/5 TrendForce disclosure on 10-30% upfront cash advances and minimum-price clauses, the structural-pricing case is now well-supported.

Pillar 3 — OP doesn't peak until 2Q28
"We do not expect OP to peak until 2Q28, with the stock typically 1-2 quarters ahead of this."

Cycle-extension validation. The 5/4 Korean brokerage cascade (Daol ₩2.1M / KI ₩2.05M / KB ₩2.0M) is now formally ratified by Western sell-side. Korean buy-side and Western sell-side aligned on cycle extension into 2028. Stock peak signal ~Q4-27 / Q1-28 — i.e., 18-24 months from here.

8. Why UBS Now Prefers Samsung Over Hynix (Critical Engagement)

  • Relative outperformance: Hynix +158% YTD vs Samsung ords +121% YTD; the gap is the source of the rotation call.
  • HBM premium contracting: Samsung qualifying for Rubin (~30% allocation per 5/20 NVDA disclosure) erodes Hynix's HBM exclusivity.
  • Capacity headroom: Samsung has more available capacity for share-take; Hynix capacity-bound until Yongin Y1 (Feb 2027).
  • Foundry optionality: Samsung has agentic-AI silicon ramp; Hynix doesn't.
  • Multiple ceiling: Hynix already at 3.57× NTM P/BV (close to historical peak).

What this does not say: UBS is not calling the memory cycle peak. The vehicle-selection question is what changed.

9. Critical Engagement — Weakest Links

Risk 1 DRAM ASP forecast is heroic

+186% in 2026, +37% in 2027 on top. Bit-shipment growth +26%/+17% in the same window. At 80% OP margin, every 10pp lower ASP YoY drops OP margin ~6-8pp on the same revenue base. Zero margin of error on pricing.

Falsification: If 3Q26 DDR contract pricing prints flat or negative QoQ (vs UBS +40% QoQ trajectory), 2027E EPS needs to come down 20-30%.

Risk 2 LT ROE 40.1% step is the multiple-expanding lever — but it's a big claim

Justified by HBM consuming 25-31% of industry DRAM capacity through 2027. If 2028+ China brings 1b nm DRAM online OR CXMT delivers HBM3 at scale, the industry capacity arithmetic changes. The 40% ROE depends on supply discipline holding — which depends on continued export controls.

Risk 3 $1.4T industry revenue 2027 is flagged but not modeled

For context, total global semiconductor revenue 2024 was ~$600B. UBS implicitly models memory alone at >2x the total 2024 semis market by 2027. The affordability constraint has to bite somewhere in the customer P&L; UBS doesn't model where. Big 4 hyperscaler 2026 capex aggregate is $710B; even at 30% memory content allocation, ~$213B — math works for 2026, harder for 2027's $1.4T.

Risk 4 UBS doesn't engage with the alternative-memory bear case

CXMT HBM3 end-2026 (Bloomberg 3/26), China policy-supported memory subsidization, NVIDIA's HBM4 dual-tier qualification (lowering the bar to broaden supply) — all unaddressed.

Risk 5 Single-point-of-failure persists across UBS calls

Same UBS team runs SK Hynix + Samsung + memory complex. The 1.55MM wpm CoWoS model on the TSM / NVDA / AVGO side and the 500k/690k wpm HBM capacity model on the memory side share a common HBM TAM assumption. If HBM TAM is wrong, multiple UBS calls fail simultaneously.

Risk 6 The 2029E down-cycle is convenient

UBS embeds −21% revenue / −29% OP in 2029E with DRAM ASP −30%. This is the cyclical reset that keeps the model defensible. Worth asking: what's the falsifiable claim about WHY 2029 specifically vs 2028 or 2030?

12. Net Take

The hypothesis side: Very strong supporting evidence. Memory layer being repriced as structural AI infra (40% LT ROE = utility-like, not cycle-like). UBS framing of "almost all incremental DRAM capacity going to HBM, no new NAND capacity ex China" is the cleanest articulation of the binding-constraint thesis at this layer.

The investment side: Hynix Friday close ₩1.94M against PT ₩2.25M = +15.9% to base PT. Upside/downside ratio 2.4:1 at current price — still attractive but no longer the 4:1 it was in Feb. UBS's preference rotation to Samsung is worth taking seriously: Hynix has run on HBM exclusivity premium that's now contracting as Samsung qualifies for Rubin. If memory-cycle thesis is correct, Samsung now has more share-take optionality near-term.

What to watch: (i) 3Q26 DDR contract pricing — if it prints flat-or-negative QoQ vs UBS +40% trajectory, the model breaks. (ii) ADR launch and pricing (UBS catalyst date June 3 2026 referenced in QRR). (iii) Samsung Tier 2 file initiation question — are we missing material upside by not having direct Samsung coverage? (iv) Next UBS Micron note for whether the +40% LT ROE framework extends across the memory complex.
