Computational Abundance — Strongest Single-Session Confirmation

Four most consequential datapoints for the supporting-evidence tracker:

  1. GOOG Cloud backlog ~doubled to $462B in one quarter. ~50% to convert within 24 months ⇒ ~$230B contracted demand recognized over the next 8 quarters from GCP alone.
  2. AWS Trainium revenue commitments >$225B, plus $364B core AWS backlog (excl. Anthropic). Custom silicon is itself now a $225B+ contracted business — additive to GPU spend, not substitutive.
  3. Pichai + Hood explicit "compute-constrained through 2026." Two of three biggest cloud spenders explicitly tying revenue underdelivery to supply, not demand — the textbook abundance-era signal.
  4. Aggregate $710B 2026 capex with 2027 guided "significantly higher." The capex curve is not flattening — it is steepening into 2027.

Component-price contribution (~$25B of MSFT's $190B; primary driver of META's range raise) is a second-order abundance signal: input markets (memory) are responding to demand by tightening prices — reinforces P015 and Micron/SK Hynix pricing power.
