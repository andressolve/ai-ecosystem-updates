Overnight Developments

bear TSMC's beat-and-raise triggers the cycle's sharpest selloff

TSMC reported Q2 2026 results on 7/16: revenue NT$1,270.38B, EPS NT$27.25, gross margin 67.7% — beating even the top of its own 65.5-67.5% guide. Management raised FY26 USD revenue growth guidance to "slightly above 40%" YoY (from "above 30%," last raised 4/16 and reiterated 5/28), raised 2026 capex guidance to $60-64B (from $52-56B), and announced a new $100B investment commitment in Arizona. Q3 gross margin was guided at a 65% floor, with management explicitly quantifying N2 ramp dilution of 3-4 percentage points — the first disclosed N2 cost drag this cycle. The CEO described the multi-year capex trajectory as "even more significantly higher," not flagging any near-term peak.

Despite this, the market reaction was the worst of the cycle: TSM ADR premarket -5.85% to $394.95, the Taiwan primary listing (2330.TW) -7.29% in its own 7/17 session, and the selloff spread across the complex — AVGO -7.30%, ASML -4.29%, MU -8.22%, SK Hynix -11.53% (Seoul, 7/16 session). The read is capex-sustainability and crowded-positioning concern, not demand disconfirmation — see the BofA item below. P010 upgraded to 98%, P011 to 86%; a new prediction (P031, 70%) was logged specifically to test whether the new, tighter "40%+" guidance floor itself holds through year-end, since the original ~30% bar has now been overtaken by TSMC's own numbers.

watch BofA: "Long Global Semiconductors" is now the most crowded trade globally

BofA's fund-manager survey found "Long Global Semiconductors" has overtaken prior "Long Magnificent 7" readings as the single most crowded trade worldwide. The sector has shed an estimated ~$3.3T of aggregate market value since June 22 even as the underlying fundamental prints (TSMC, ASML, Micron) improved over the same window. This is logged explicitly as a market-structure watch item, not disconfirming evidence — extreme positioning can drive continued volatility independent of the physical-demand thesis, and conflating the two would be exactly the kind of confirmation-bias-prone read this repo is built to avoid. Today's selloff is being read as largely positioning-driven.

NBIS fell -16.40% premarket to $166.80, more than double CoreWeave's -8.36% move in the same complex-wide selloff — a magnitude that points to a Nebius-specific overlay on top of the broader tape. Discovery-feed reporting flagged a newly-disclosed asset-light capacity-partnership structure drawing investor scrutiny over monetization quality and revenue/capex accounting treatment. This has not been independently verified against a primary source (8-K/IR release) in this sweep — logged for follow-up next session rather than analyzed further today. No probability changes (no active NBIS-specific predictions in the log).

neutral Memory competitive-threat set widens: CXMT and SK Hynix capacity plans

Two new structural threats to the memory pricing-power thesis surfaced this window, distinct from the demand side (P015/P028 unaffected): CXMT (China's state-backed DRAM maker) is advancing an IPO/capacity-expansion push — the first credible China-domestic DRAM supply threat logged this cycle — and SK Hynix disclosed its own plan to roughly double capacity by 2030. Neither is evidence of near-term oversupply; both are inputs to whether the "structurally scarce supply supports a premium multiple" logic (the P029 thesis) holds through 12/31/26. Against a reconciled $98.52 FY27E consensus EPS, MU's forward multiple is now ~8.42x at $829.92 premarket — a new cycle low.

neutral ASML order visibility extends to 2027-2028; Japan announces sovereign AI buildout

ASML's Q2 beat came with, per Citi, 2027 and 2028 orders already in hand — extending the confirmed-demand horizon two years past what was visible before the print, equipment-layer confirmation that the buildout isn't a near-term pull-forward. Separately, Japan announced a national sovereign-AI infrastructure commitment (27,500 NVIDIA Rubin GPUs) — a new demand-driver category (state-level compute procurement) distinct from hyperscaler capex, worth watching for whether other sovereigns follow.
