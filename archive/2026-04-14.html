What Happened

Hormuz Blockade: Targeted, Not Full — Market Relief Rally

What changed vs. yesterday: CENTCOM clarified that the blockade applies ONLY to ships entering/leaving Iranian ports and coastal areas. "Will not impede freedom of navigation for vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz to and from non-Iranian ports." Yesterday's Lloyd's List "all commercial vessels stopped" framing was wrong.

Market response: Oil crashed — WTI from $103.40 → $92.34 (-10.6%). S&P wiped out all Iran war losses. Nasdaq +0.93%. Markets treating this as a negotiation tactic, not permanent escalation.

Diplomatic signals: Trump says Iran "called and wants to work a deal." JD Vance led 21+ hours of face-to-face talks in Islamabad that failed, but the door isn't closed. Second round of talks possible before April 21 ceasefire expiration.

Key remaining binary: April 21 ceasefire expiration. If ceasefire expires without renewal AND oil re-spikes >$105 → P008 back to 73%. If deal reached → 82–85%.

Meta Muse Spark — Jevons Paradox in One Data Point

First model from Meta Superintelligence Labs (Alexandr Wang, brought in 9 months ago). Key claim: achieves same capability as Llama 4 mid-size for "an order of magnitude less compute." Natively multimodal reasoning with tool use, visual chain of thought, multi-agent orchestration.

Why it matters: The "10x less compute" claim is the strongest single data point on the efficiency side of the Jevons paradox debate. If Meta can match prior capability at 10x lower cost, why wouldn't total compute demand decline?

Because Meta is simultaneously committing $35.2B to CoreWeave + $115–135B in own capex. The efficiency gains are being deployed to serve 3.4B users across more surfaces (WhatsApp, glasses, Instagram DMs), not to reduce spend. Textbook Jevons paradox: cheaper compute → more use cases → more total compute demand.

For computational abundance thesis: Jevons paradox holding. Supporting evidence with the efficiency caveat logged as a monitoring item.
