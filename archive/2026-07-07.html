What Happened

watch Samsung Q2 preliminary: beat on profit, missed on revenue, stock fell ~7% anyway

Samsung's preliminary Q2 print: operating profit ₩89.4T (~$58.4B, +1,800% YoY off a depressed base) BEAT the ~₩85.5–87.3T consensus range; revenue ₩171T (+28% QoQ) MISSED consensus (~₩172.18T, ~0.7% light). Stock closed down ~7% (KOSPI briefly halted) despite the record profit. This is the exact referee flagged in yesterday's report — and it resolves as neither a clean confirmation nor a clean disconfirmation. Two real-but-bounded factors drove the selloff: (1) Samsung's plan to spend ~₩400T (~$270B+) on a new semiconductor manufacturing hub pressures near-term FCF (a financing-fragility angle, not a demand cut); (2) the revenue miss — small in magnitude (~0.7%) but the first sub-consensus top-line print anywhere in the memory complex this cycle — is worth tracking, not yet a trend. Operating profit, the cleaner AI-memory-margin signal, beat decisively, so DRAM-undersupply evidence (P015) is unaffected. Contagion spread to European chip equipment: ASML fell 3.6–5.1%, STMicro −3.6%, Infineon −4.6%, ASMI/BESI ~−5%, Soitec −13.8% — the market is now trading the entire semi-cap-equipment complex as a single beta to the memory story. Source: CNBC, Yahoo Finance, wccftech, ainvest (ASML contagion)

bull Custom silicon proliferation widens to a 4th and 5th player — DeepSeek and Anthropic

Reuters reports DeepSeek has spent roughly a year developing its own in-house inference chip (not training), talking to foundry and memory partners, to reduce Nvidia/Huawei dependence on its fastest-growing (inference) workload — still early-stage, pre-tapeout. Separately (breaking 7/2, caught late here — IBKR down that day), The Information and Bloomberg reported Anthropic is in early talks with Samsung to manufacture a custom AI chip on Samsung's 2nm process + advanced packaging, having hired Clive Chan (ex-OpenAI custom-chip team); Anthropic explicitly says NVDA GPUs, Google TPUs, and AWS Trainium remain "central" to its compute strategy — this supplements, not replaces. Together with Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Meta MTIA, and OpenAI's Jalapeño (Broadcom, unveiled 6/24), five major buyers are now building or discussing custom silicon. This is squarely confirming for sub-thesis #4 (custom silicon as an additive, co-equal pillar) — the compute base is now large enough that every major buyer wants its own chip path — but the market reflexively sold NVDA (~−2% pre-market on the DeepSeek headline) as if each new custom-chip project is a direct substitution threat, which is the value-capture (sub-thesis #5) reflex, distinct from the demand signal. Source: Reuters via US News, TechCrunch (Anthropic-Samsung), Bloomberg
