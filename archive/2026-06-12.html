What Happened

bull NVIDIA opens Vera CPU order-taking to Chinese clients — a channel GPUs can't take

Reuters (exclusive, 6/12, corroborated by TrendForce, US News, Seeking Alpha) reports NVIDIA has told Chinese clients its new Vera data-center CPU could be available as soon as August and they can begin placing orders. One major Chinese cloud provider is reportedly planning to order >300 servers, each with two Vera processors (~600+ CPUs). The strategic point, per the reporting: CPUs face materially less U.S. export-control friction than GPUs, so Vera opens a China revenue path that the stalled H200 cannot use.

Why it matters. Two reads, both modestly positive:

  1. P027 optionality (held 75%). P027 (NVDA Vera CPU CY26 ≥$15B, = 75% of Kress's ~$20B visibility) was built on a US/RoW base. A China channel is incremental optionality that was never in the bull case — directionally supportive, but the disclosed order (~600 CPUs; at a guessed ~$10–15K ASP that's only ~$6–9M) is far too small to move the 75%. The signal is the path existing, not the dollar size. Held 75%.
  2. Abundance / agentic-CPU demand (confirming, small). Vera is NVIDIA's CPU "purpose-built for agentic AI" (head-node / KV-cache / orchestration). Demand showing up even inside an export-constrained market is a small confirming datapoint for the CPU-demand-as-agentic-compute read (cf. the 5/19 CPU special report, AMZN-Snowflake Graviton 5/28). Mix-shift, not aggregate — consistent with prior framing.

Bear/caveat: sourced to "people familiar," not NVIDIA IR; order interest ≠ bookings; and selling compute into China invites the same export-control reversal risk that just stalled the H200. Lead, not a logged datapoint.
