What Happened

1. NVIDIA GTC 2026 — $1 Trillion Order Pipeline very strong

Jensen disclosed $1T in purchase orders for Blackwell + Vera Rubin through 2027 — double the $500B figure from GTC 2025. For context, NVIDIA's FY25 revenue was ~$130B, so this pipeline equals ~8 years of recent revenue compressed into 2 years.

  • 7 chips in full production, 5 rack systems announced — densest single product announcement in NVIDIA's history
  • Orders cover chips, networking, and CPUs only (not software/services)
  • Single largest demand visibility figure in semiconductor history
Bull: $1T validates the AI infrastructure buildout is structural, not cyclical. Pipeline equals ~8x annual revenue — unprecedented demand visibility for a semiconductor company. Even 70% conversion is transformative.
Bear: How much is firm POs vs. aspirational commitments? Jensen said "purchase orders" which implies firm, but the magnitude warrants skepticism. At $4.48T market cap, further multiple expansion requires revenue materialization, not pipeline announcements.

2. Groq 3 LPU — NVIDIA's Inference Answer new product

The most strategically significant new product at GTC. The Groq 3 LPU is NVIDIA's first dedicated inference chip, derived from the $20B Groq acquisition (Dec 2025).

  • 256 LPUs per LPX rack, 128GB SRAM per processor, 640 TB/s scale-up bandwidth
  • 35x more tokens per megawatt and 10x more revenue opportunity for trillion-parameter models vs Blackwell when paired with NVL72
  • Ships H2 2026
  • Directly addresses low-latency inference market where Cerebras and others were competing

NVIDIA now offers training (GPU) + inference (LPU) as an integrated platform. Customers get inference-optimized hardware within the NVIDIA ecosystem — no need for third-party inference silicon.

3. Vera CPU Standalone — Entering the CPU Market new market

88-core Olympus ARM architecture. 1.2 TB/s memory bandwidth per socket — 3x Intel's top 6900P (825 GB/s) and 2x AMD Turin (560-600 GB/s). Up to 1.5TB LPDDR5X. 900 GB/s NVLink-C2C.

  • Vera CPU Rack: 256 liquid-cooled CPUs, 64 BlueField-4 DPUs, 22,500+ cores, 400TB aggregate memory
  • Committed deployers: Alibaba, ByteDance, Meta, Oracle, CoreWeave, Lambda, Nebius, NScale
  • Targets the $35-40B data center CPU TAM
  • Positioned for agentic AI workloads (web browsing, data processing, tool use) that require CPU compute alongside GPU inference

NVIDIA was previously only in the CPU market via Grace paired with GPUs. Vera as a standalone CPU goes directly after Intel and AMD in the data center. If successful, this opens an entirely new TAM.

4. Vera Rubin Platform Confirmed in Production confirmed

First Vera Rubin NVL72 already running inside Microsoft Azure — Satya Nadella confirmed publicly.

  • AWS deploying 1M+ GPUs and LPUs starting 2026
  • 10x inference throughput/watt, 1/10th cost per token vs Blackwell
  • NVL72 needs 1/4 the GPUs for large MoE models vs Blackwell
  • Vera Rubin platform: 7 chips (Rubin GPU 336B transistors, Vera CPU, NVLink 6, ConnectX-9, BlueField-4, Spectrum-6, Groq 3 LPU)

Vera Rubin Ultra NVL576 (2027): Kyber rack architecture — 144 GPUs in vertical compute trays, 8 NVL72 racks connected via copper + direct optical into a 576-GPU NVLink domain.

6. Feynman (2028) — Manufacturing Diversification roadmap

NVIDIA's first manufacturing diversification away from TSMC. ~25% of production potentially moving to Intel Foundry.

  • TSMC A16 (1.6nm) for GPU compute die
  • Intel 14A for I/O die + ~25% of packaging via EMIB
  • Rosa CPU (named for Rosalind Franklin), LP40 LPU, BlueField-5, CX10, NVLink-8
  • Custom HBM (beyond HBM4E), silicon photonics, 13+ TB/s memory bandwidth

Signals: (a) demand so large a single foundry may be insufficient, (b) geopolitical risk mitigation (Taiwan concentration). If Intel 14A slips, does this create a Feynman delay?
