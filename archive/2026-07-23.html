bear Alphabet Q2: strong Cloud/capex beat, sharpest selloff of the cycle

Alphabet's Q2 2026 print (7/22 AMC): revenue $119.8B (+24% YoY), Cloud $24.8B (+82% YoY, accelerating from Q1's 63%), Cloud backlog +$50B QoQ to $514B, operating income +30% to $40.8B. Management flagged Q3 Cloud margin pressure from leaning on third-party capacity as a bridge, extending the 7/21 "Frozen chip" report of Google Cloud turning away business from internal compute shortage. Stock fell -6.55% premarket ($323.50) — the sharpest single-print reaction of the cycle — dragging META (-5.3%), AMZN (-4.2%), and MSFT (-2.5%) down on shared "AI spending concerns." This is a materially different reaction function than the 4/29 print, which rallied on the same "compute-constrained" framing; worth watching whether this is GOOG-specific (the new FCF-negative data point) or a regime shift in how the market prices hyperscaler capex. Note: the "memory/chips rally while the capex-payer sells off" pattern does not clearly hold here — Micron (same US premarket session) was roughly flat (-0.9%), undercutting a clean cross-stack divergence read. bull AMD-Anthropic deal confirmed: $5B equity + up to 2GW MI450/Helios

The deal flagged as unconfirmed yesterday was confirmed at AMD's Advancing AI 2026 event (7/22 PM): AMD is investing up to $5B in Anthropic (its first direct equity stake, paid out as deployment targets are met), paired with Anthropic committing to deploy up to 2GW of AMD Instinct MI450-series GPUs on Helios rack systems (MI455X + EPYC "Venice" + Pensando + ROCm). First 1GW tranche arrives H1 2027. AMD stock spiked ~10% intraday to ~$553 before paring back to $525.19 premarket (-4.29%) — a partial give-back, not a reversal. Anthropic's compute stack is now TPU (Google) + Trainium (Amazon) + AMD (confirmed third vendor) — a genuine diversification datapoint for sub-thesis #4 (custom/multi-vendor silicon), though it doesn't move P003 (Anthropic remains TPU/Trainium-primary; 2GW is additive at this stage, not a training-compute-majority shift). Sources: artificialintelligence-news.com, CRN Asia.

watch Arista: "demand is outstripping our supply" — multi-year, not transient

Arista raised its 2026 revenue forecast to $11.5B (consistent with the 5/5 print) but CEO Jayshree Ullal issued an explicit multi-year (1-2 year, not transient) supply warning spanning wafers, silicon chips, CPUs, optics, and memory, with gross margin pressure from paying more to secure parts. This is a third independent same-week signal — alongside GOOG's Cloud margin commentary and the 7/21 "Frozen chip" report — that supply is not keeping pace with demand across cloud, networking, and internal-compute layers simultaneously.
