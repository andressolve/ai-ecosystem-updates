Brent ~$114 WTI ~$103 UAE OPEC exit May 1, 2026 Hormuz reopening 2H26 per Baker Hughes
  • Trump rejected Iran proposal 4/27; oil broke decisively above $110 Brent and held there.
  • UAE announces departure from OPEC + OPEC+ effective May 1, 2026 — first major OPEC defection in decades. UAE = OPEC's #3 producer (~12% supply); 60 yrs of membership. Stated reason: "national interest" and frustration with quotas.

Direct AI-infrastructure transmission channels: (1) data center electricity opex (5–15% of opex line) elevated for 18–24 months; (2) Hormuz disruption affects Qatar helium (~1/3 world supply, critical for litho), Korean/Taiwanese fab LNG power supply, Gulf copper/aluminum exports — physical supply chain constraints on the buildout independent of oil price. Tonight's hyperscaler call is the test of whether energy has crossed into capex/margin commentary.
