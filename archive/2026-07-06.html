watch Caught late: NVIDIA's DSX revenue-share / vendor-financing model

Broke 7/1-7/2, missed in the 7/2 and 7/3 reports (IBKR down; it surfaced via NVIDIA's own blog + CNBC, not the wire feed). NVIDIA formalized a revenue-sharing + credit-support model for AI cloud operators building "DSX AI factories": NVIDIA backstops neocloud GPU buildouts with credit support and takes a recurring cut of the cloud revenue that capacity generates — on top of standard hardware margin (two income streams per deal). Participating clouds draw token credits against future capacity now ("compute now, pay later"). Launch partners:

  • Sharon AI — up to 40,000 GB300 GPUs, 6-year deal, 72 MW of new Australian capacity.
  • Firmus — DSX campus in Batam, Indonesia, scaling to 360 MW / up to 170,000 GPUs, with $25-30B in committed offtake over the deal's first six years.
  • Combined: ~210,000 GPUs.

The DSX blueprint debuted at Computex (6/1); this is the commercial financing model layered on it. Two-sided, logged Weak-Moderate (watch):

  • (a) Abundance-confirming. Vendor financing unlocks the buildout for capital-constrained neoclouds, expanding the deployment/inference surface. You don't extend multi-year credit + revenue-share against capacity you don't expect to be utilized — it's a demand-conviction signal from the party with the best demand visibility.
  • (b) Value-capture + financing-fragility (sub-thesis #5). NVIDIA extends its pricing power beyond hardware into recurring cloud economics — capturing margin the pure-play neoclouds (CoreWeave/Nebius) would otherwise keep (same value-capture vector as Meta Compute, 7/2). And it joins the circular-financing pattern: the chip vendor now helps fund its own customers' purchases — the same single-point-of-failure family as AVGO/Apollo-Blackstone (P024 watch-item) and the 6/23 leverage unwind. To the extent revenue is financing-dependent, it is a lower-quality demand signal.

Why weighted low: modest dollar amounts vs the ~$710B hyperscaler capex base; opt-in structure; and vendor financing is standard in capital-goods cycles (turns lumpy capex into recurring revenue, improving NVDA's own economics — arguably a rebuttal to the SemiAnalysis pricing-power critique, since NVDA now monetizes utilization, not just silicon). No prob shift. Monitoring trigger: the rev-share/credit book scales to a material fraction of NVDA DC revenue, OR a financed neocloud defaults / GPU rental rates fall → escalate.
