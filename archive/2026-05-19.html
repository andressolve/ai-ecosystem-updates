What Happened

1. Google-Blackstone $5B TPU JV (BXN1) — dominant signal (5/18 PM / 5/19 AM)

The announcement: Blackstone and Google launched a standalone company (codename BXN1) that will sell Google's TPUs as compute-as-a-service to outside customers, in addition to access via Google Cloud. Blackstone contributes $5B equity for a majority stake; total deal value ~$25B with leverage. First 500MW of capacity targeted online in 2027. Jas Khaira — who previously led Blackstone's CoreWeave investment — heads the unit.

Why this is the most important strategic disclosure since 4/29:

  1. Sub-thesis #4 (custom silicon co-equal pillar) validated at the capital-markets layer. Institutional capital pricing TPU silicon as worth selling at scale to non-Google buyers. The merchant TPU policy announced 4/29 now has a specific, capitalized, leadership-named vehicle within ~3 weeks.
  2. Cross-Blackstone signal — most loaded cross-stack data point of 2026 to date. Blackstone concurrently anchoring TWO distinct AI-silicon paths: $35B private-credit lead arranger for AVGO custom-XPU buildout (5/8 mandate ratified through 5/11) AND majority equity sponsor for BXN1 (5/18). Same private-credit franchise routing capital across both custom-silicon paths = revealed preference that the accelerator market is multi-vendor / not zero-sum.
  3. Direct competitive shot at CoreWeave + Nebius. BXN1 is purpose-built to challenge the GPU-cloud neocloud cohort at the lower-cost TPU layer for inference. Market priced this immediately: CRWV −6.8% / NBIS −10.75% intraday on 5/19. The Khaira lead is especially loaded: Blackstone's prior CRWV-investment lead now runs structural competitor with same capital base = market-revealed preference of merchant TPU over incremental neocloud GPU exposure.
  4. AVGO captures silicon margin regardless of who owns the box. Google's TPU is AVGO-designed (Tomahawk + Jericho networking + co-designed XPU). Whether the chip is consumed via Google Cloud captive, GOOG merchant TPU direct-sales (4/29), or BXN1 standalone vehicle, AVGO earns the silicon margin per chip. BXN1 actually expands AVGO TPU demand by opening a third procurement channel.
  5. NVDA terminal-share-of-spend question compressed further. Compresses the 2027+ accelerator pie share; doesn't compress 2026 NVDA demand (already locked in via $1T backlog + hyperscaler capex commitments). Combined with AMD Helios/MI450 6 GW + Anthropic 1M TPUs + AWS Trainium $225B = multi-vendor accelerator pie is now structurally larger AND NVDA's share within it is being incrementally diluted.
