What Happened

1. Meta-Nebius $27B Data Center Deal very strong infrastructure

Meta signed a $27B, 5-year agreement with Nebius to build and operate data center infrastructure using Vera Rubin GPUs. One of the largest single AI infrastructure contracts in history.

  • ~$5.4B/year average contract value — approaches Nebius's entire 2026 revenue guide ($3.0-3.4B)
  • Extends Meta's outsourcing strategy: $14.2B CoreWeave + $27B Nebius = $41.2B committed to independent GPU cloud
  • Every dollar flows to NVIDIA (GPUs), SK Hynix/Samsung (HBM), TSMC (manufacturing)
  • Validates independent GPU cloud as a durable demand channel alongside hyperscaler in-house buildout
Bull: Meta committing $41.2B to external GPU cloud providers (CoreWeave + Nebius) on top of $115-135B internal capex. Total Meta AI infrastructure commitment exceeds $150B. Demand is so large that even the hyperscaler with the most aggressive in-house buildout needs overflow capacity.
Bear: META stock -6.4%. Market views every incremental infrastructure commitment as earnings dilutive. The 4:1 capex-to-AI-revenue ratio remains the unresolved question. How long before these investments generate measurable returns?

2. NVIDIA $2B Investment in Nebius strategic

NVIDIA invested $2B in Nebius, extending its invest-in-customers flywheel (CoreWeave, Anthropic, OpenAI, now Nebius). Nebius targeting 5+ GW of compute capacity through 2030.

The flywheel: NVIDIA capital → customer capacity → chip demand → NVIDIA revenue. Each investment creates a captive GPU buyer. Combined with Meta's $27B deal, Nebius is now both NVIDIA-backed and Meta-anchored — a fundamentally stronger position than 30 days ago.

3. Google: Wiz ($32B) + Intersect Power ($4.75B) m&a

  • Wiz ($32B): Largest cybersecurity acquisition ever. Cloud security for GCP. Strengthens competitive position vs AWS/Azure.
  • Intersect Power ($4.75B): Power asset acquisition for DC buildout. Google vertically integrating into energy — consistent with White House Ratepayer Protection Pledge.

The Intersect deal is the more interesting signal: hyperscalers are buying, not leasing, their power infrastructure. This is bullish for buildout pace (secure long-term power supply) but raises concentration risk for independent power producers.

4. Micron at $448.20 — Post-FQ2 Breakout market signal

MU +8.7% from pre-earnings level (~$412). Market digesting FQ2 beat ($23.86B, +28% vs guide) and FQ3 guide ($33.5B at 81% GM). At $448.20, MU trades at 5.3x FY27E EPS (UBS $83.80).

SK Hynix at &won;1,007,000 (-5.1%) — same underlying memory cycle but Korea discount persists. SK Hynix trades at 2.9x 2027E EPS vs Micron's 5.3x — a 45% valuation discount for the company with 52% HBM share and 60-70% NVIDIA HBM4 allocation.

5. CoreWeave Under Pressure — $85.27 (-12.6%) watch

Sustained weakness after Q1 guide miss ($1.9-2.0B vs $2.3B consensus), Q4 $452M net loss, and Blue Owl financing failure. Securities fraud lead plaintiff deadline passed (March 13) — litigation will proceed but is likely ambulance-chasing.

Competitive angle: Meta spreading GPU cloud spending ($14.2B CoreWeave + $27B Nebius). CoreWeave's leverage ($21.4B debt) vs Nebius's NVIDIA backing ($2B investment) is a competitive disadvantage in securing capital.

Open Questions
