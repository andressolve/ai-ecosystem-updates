Bull counter (E7) May 1 launch of E7 ($99/seat) bundles Copilot so deeply into M365 that enterprises can't easily separate it. If penetration moves from 3% to even 10-15% of 450M seats, incremental revenue is $4-8B/yr. E7 = the designed fix.

2. SUPPORTING — NVIDIA + Emerald AI Create "Flexible AI Factories as Grid Assets" supporting power constraint

NVIDIA and Emerald AI partnered with six of the largest US energy companies to build AI data centers that operate as flexible grid assets — modulating power consumption to support grid stability.

  • Partners: AES, Constellation Energy, Invenergy, NextEra Energy, Nscale Energy & Power, Vistra
  • Concept: AI data centers participate in demand-response programs, becoming controllable loads that help balance the grid
  • Significance: Repositions AI operators as grid solutions rather than grid burdens — potentially accelerating permitting and grid connection timelines (currently 3-7 years)
Bull Addresses the most persistent buildout bottleneck creatively. If hyperscalers and GPU clouds adopt this model at scale, power access timelines compress. Data centers become infrastructure assets, not just load.
Bear Demand-response means you must curtail compute when the grid needs it. AI workloads (training runs, inference serving) have stringent uptime requirements. The flexibility the grid needs may not be compatible with the reliability AI customers require.

Source: NVIDIA/Emerald AI press release, Dow Jones wire (March 23, 2026)

3. NEW DEMAND VECTOR — Elon Musk Announces Terafab: $25B Chip Factory in Austin demand signal execution risk

Musk formally announced "Terafab" — a joint Tesla/SpaceX/xAI semiconductor factory in Austin, Texas.

  • Cost: $25 billion estimated
  • Target: "Terawatt of computing power per year" (ambitious framing)
  • Chips for: Tesla autonomous vehicles, Optimus humanoid robots, xAI Grok AI
  • Ambition: Compete with Samsung and TSMC on leading-edge nodes
  • Market reaction: ASML surged (future equipment customer); Tesla stock muted (investors skeptical)
For the thesis Validates that physical AI (robots, autonomous vehicles) requires chip supply at a scale that justifies building your own fab. Whether Terafab works or not, the demand signal is real: Tesla/SpaceX believe they will need chips at massive scale.
Execution reality Musk companies have zero semiconductor manufacturing experience. TSMC took decades. Intel's foundry ambition, with 50 years of experience, has struggled. Terafab is realistically 5-10 years from meaningful production, with high probability of scope reduction or cancellation.

Source: Barrons.com, IBD (March 22-23, 2026)

Thesis Status

Computational Abundance Hypothesis — Moderate-High, Holding

New supporting evidence: NVIDIA grid partnership (addresses power constraint creatively), Zuckerberg agent-to-agent communication in production (multiplicative inference demand signal).

New disconfirming evidence: Microsoft Copilot crisis (3% M365 penetration) is the clearest data point yet that enterprise AI monetization is NOT automatic, even with maximum distribution advantage.

Critical distinction: The Copilot crisis is about monetization difficulty, not about compute demand. Microsoft is burning more Azure capacity to fix Copilot, not less. The demand is real; the revenue conversion is hard. These are different risks for different investors:

Infrastructure (NVDA, TSM, MU) Even if AI monetizes poorly, compute demand is real and growing. Microsoft trains more models when Copilot fails, not fewer. Infrastructure demand is upstream of monetization.
Application / platform (MSFT, AI SaaS) If enterprise AI doesn't monetize, hyperscaler AI capex justification weakens over 12-24 months. Eventually, CFOs ask why they're spending $140B/yr on Azure AI infrastructure if AI revenue isn't growing proportionally.

Open Questions

1. What is NVIDIA's "new China problem"? Barrons headline March 23: "NVIDIA Stock Is Stuck — and It's Facing a New China Problem." China H200 production halted (March 11 entry), pipeline marked to $0-2B. The "new" problem is unclear — likely post-halt complications (export license revocation risk, customer relations, Ascend competitive pressure). Investigate before NVDA Q1 FY27 earnings in May.
2. Is the E7 bundle the Copilot fix, or does it just hide a failing product? The 18.8% → 11.5% enterprise paid share decline means early adopters are leaving, not just new buyers not arriving. Bundling doesn't solve retention if the product experience is poor. Need Q3 FY26 Microsoft earnings (late April) for any early E7 signal, and Q2 FY27 (July) for the definitive read.
3. Terafab — real or vaporware? Monitoring signals: (a) Austin land permits, (b) ASML equipment orders from Terafab entity, (c) TSMC or Samsung announcing foundry partnership. No major chip factory has succeeded without decades of semiconductor experience. Realistic first wafer: 5-7 years at best.
4. OpenAI advertising — direct competitor or infrastructure shift? If OpenAI competes directly with Meta and Google in advertising, these relationships become adversarial. OpenAI is currently backed by Microsoft (~23% equity) and has partnerships with Google. If advertising revenue takes off, the AI ecosystem's competitive dynamics change fundamentally in 2027-28.
