Open Questions

1. How binding is the TSMC capacity constraint on NVIDIA's Vera Rubin ramp? Broadcom's exec says TSMC is "hitting limits" but NVIDIA has preferential allocation. Does preferential mean unaffected, or does even NVIDIA face marginal constraints at these demand levels? Jensen's Q1 earnings commentary will be critical.
2. Will the Anthropic injunction ruling create a buying opportunity? If the preliminary injunction is granted, Anthropic's commercial trajectory is de-risked. Primarily affects competitive dynamics between Claude and Copilot, and the broader government AI market.
3. PCB lead times 6 weeks → 6 months — who is most exposed? Under-covered bottleneck. Optical transceivers need PCBs. Data center networking needs optical transceivers. If PCB lead times don't compress, networking buildout is constrained even if chip supply improves. Arista and Broadcom networking divisions worth monitoring.
4. Is the Micron selloff (-16% in 7 sessions) a buying opportunity? Fundamentals are the strongest in Micron's 40-year history. FQ3 guide $33.5B at 81% GM. Daiwa PT $700. At $397, ~4.7x FY26E P/S and ~8.8x FY26E PE. The computational abundance thesis says this time is structurally different. But memory cycles historically surprise to the downside.
