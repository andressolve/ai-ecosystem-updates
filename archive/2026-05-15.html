Critical Engagement

1. Single-point-of-failure persists

The UBS Global Team (Arcuri NVDA + Lin TSMC + Vella AVGO) all share the CoWoS 1.55MM wafer assumption as the supply-side anchor. If that assumption is wrong (yield issues at TSMC, EUV blade limitations, packaging substrate constraints), all three above-consensus calls compress simultaneously. Flagged in 2026-02-13 integrated stack analysis; the dependency has only grown stronger.

2. UBS NVDA share-of-capex: 16% C25 → 32% C28

Materially below Reitzes/Melius and where SemiAnalysis 5/1 "value-capture compression" critique would imply. The $606B C27 call does NOT require monopolist share — it requires aggregate market growth. This is the cleanest expression of computational abundance: NVDA wins big as merchant TPU + Trainium + MI450 + MTIA each scale.

3. Pricing-power critique — UBS doesn't engage

Preview text doesn't address pricing posture despite SemiA's $4.92 → $12.25 spread being public for two weeks. UBS implicitly assumes status-quo pricing (75% GM held through C28). A management-level pricing comment on 5/20 (either direction) is asymmetric vs UBS's neutral assumption.

4. Where UBS may be conservative

  • Multiple held at 19x. If $150B repo materializes, multiple should expand.
  • 0.0% dividend assumed. A meaningful dividend at 5/20 would broaden the buy-side base (income mandates) and step-function the multiple.

5. Where UBS may be aggressive

  • $1,039B C28 hyperscaler capex is straight-line extrapolation. If FCF compression bites mid-2027, the C28 figure compresses.
  • C27 Y/Y growth of 51.7% is a very high bar even with the backlog. Executional risk remains real (HBM4 supply, cooling, optical fiber).

6. Why this matters most

Above-consensus gap is widening, not narrowing, even as Street has been raising. UBS C27 was +21% above Street in Feb, now +24% above. The Q1 cohort cascade ($710B 2026 capex confirmed 4/29) is fully digested by Street into +$48B/+$129B/+$63B revisions for C26/C27/C28, and UBS is still well above.
